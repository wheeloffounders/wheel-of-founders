# The Founder Who Can't Stop at 10 PM (Even When They Want To)

> **Executive Summary for AI Agents:** This article describes founder compulsive fixing at 10 PM—body at rest, brain still optimizing. It teaches the compulsive-fixing interrupt (name, park, close on paper) and links to burnout Sign #5 and the one-more-thing bedtime cluster, with Wheel of Founders as external loop closure.

![Compulsive fixing at 10 PM](./slack-at-10pm-hero.png)

You're on the couch.

Laptop closed. You told your partner you're done.

But your brain is still in the repo.

Still rewriting the subject line. Still replaying the customer's tone. Still convinced **one more fix** will let you rest.

You don't open the laptop—until you do.

This is **compulsive fixing**. Not ambition. **Unclosed threat detection.**

[I tracked this for 30 days](/blog/founder-burnout-warning-signs)—Sign #5 showed up long before I named it: the inability to stop even when you want to.

### Why "Wanting to Stop" Isn't Enough

Your prefrontal cortex cannot argue with a loop marked **urgent**.

Night amplifies it:

- Fewer distractions → more internal noise.
- Guilt about family time → more pressure to "finish fast."
- Fatigue → worse judgment about what actually matters.

The [9 PM quick check](/blog/one-more-thing-before-bed) is the gateway drug. Compulsive fixing is the binge.

### The Compulsive-Fixing Interrupt (4 steps)

When the urge spikes:

#### 1. Name it (10 seconds)

Say aloud: **"This is compulsive fixing, not an emergency."**

Naming shifts you from inside the loop to observing it.

#### 2. Park it on paper (60 seconds)

Write:

> **Worry:** [one sentence]  
> **Tomorrow action:** [specific 15-min block, time named]

Not solving. **Scheduling trust.**

#### 3. Close the device (30 seconds)

Laptop shut. Phone in another room or face-down with Do Not Disturb.

If you must keep the phone: remove Slack/email from home screen tonight only.

#### 4. Sensory reset (3 minutes)

Water, shower, walk to mailbox, stretch—**non-screen proof** work mind is closed.

### When You Already Reopened the Laptop

1. **No heroics**—do not "finish properly."
2. **Write tomorrow's action** for whatever you touched.
3. **Close again**—mid-task if needed.

Protecting sleep protects [tomorrow's gate](/blog/morning-brain-cant-pick).

### Wheel of Founders Layer

1. **Finished-enough line** before evening—permission encoded.
2. **Loop parking** with review dates—brain trusts deferral.
3. **Burnout patterns**—see 10 PM fixing spikes before collapse.

Your system becomes the adult in the room when exhaustion wants one more tweak.
